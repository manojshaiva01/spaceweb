Recent Developments in Space Exploration
Space exploration continues to captivate humanity, with recent advancements in technology and missions. From NASA's latest discoveries to private spaceflight initiatives, the universe remains a frontier of endless possibilities.

Dark energy is a mysterious force that makes up about 68% of the universe and is responsible for its accelerated expansion. Scientists discovered dark energy in 1998 while studying distant supernovae, which showed that the universe is not just expanding but doing so at an increasing rate.

Dark Matter is an invisible and mysterious substance that makes up about 27% of the universe. Unlike normal matter, it does not emit, absorb, or reflect light, making it undetectable directly. However, scientists know it exists because of its gravitational effects on galaxies and cosmic structures.

A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it. They form when massive stars collapse under their own gravity at the end of their life cycle.

The Sola System is a vast system consisting of the Sun, eight planets, their moons, asteroids, comets, and other celestial bodies, all bound by the Sun's gravity.
